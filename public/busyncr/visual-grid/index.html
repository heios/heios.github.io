Eviction log

    What you're looking at. Each dot is a snapshot. The four bands are the retention tiers; every band is drawn at the same width per cell, so time runs 8× slower in the 24 h band than the 3 h band, and so on. Cell boundaries inside a band are anchored to absolute time — cell = floor(timestamp / cell_width) — which is why the cells drift along with their snapshots while the tier boundaries (24 h, 4 d, 16 d) sit at fixed ages.

    The pruning rule. When a snapshot crosses into a coarser tier it claims its cell there; an older snapshot still holding the same cell is evicted — keep the newest per cell. The 16 d tier additionally keeps at most 5 snapshots: when a sixth cell opens, the oldest is dropped. On the daemon, an eviction is just “drop manifest, GC unreferenced chunks”, which is what makes this retention scheme O(manifest)-cheap.

    What falls out. With an aligned 3 h schedule every eviction happens at a fixed age: 27 h (entering the 24 h grid), 5 d (entering 4 d), 20 d (entering 16 d), and 84 d (pushed past the cap). Steady state holds 8 + 3–4 + 3–4 + 5 ≈ 19–21 snapshots — out of 8 backups per day, forever. The cap of five 16 d snapshots bounds total history at ≈ 84 days.

    Policy toggle: keep oldest per cell. Flipping the checkbox makes a cell's first snapshot hold it, and every later arrival bounces the instant it crosses the tier edge — evictions land exactly on the 24 h / 4 d / 16 d lines, and cap drops land exactly at 96 d (the grid's right edge), instead of one backup-interval past them. Steady-state cost is nearly identical (~19 vs ~20 kept), but each cell is now represented by its start rather than its end, so the same cap of 5 stretches retained history from ≈ 84 d to ≈ 96 d — at the price of the freshest tier-4 snapshot being a full 16 d cell older on average.

    Verified: the precomputed timeline used by this animation matches an independent replay of the daemon's prune-after-every-backup pass at 120 random instants for each policy (keep newest / keep oldest) × jitter combination; the 16 d tier never exceeded 5; max 22 concurrent snapshots over 200 simulated days (1600 backups, 1581 prunes per run). Timeline is a pure function of t, so scrubbing backwards un-prunes correctly. Source: heios/busyncr-visual-grid.
